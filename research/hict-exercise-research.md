## 4. Difficulty Progression Framework

### BEGINNER (0-6 months)
**Capabilities:**
- 5-12 reps or 20-30 second holds
- Focus on form and technique
- May need modifications

**Recommended Exercises:**
- Incline/Knee Push-ups
- Bodyweight Squats, Wall Sits
- Planks (20-30s)
- Glute Bridges
- Inverted Rows (upright)
- Step-back Burpees

---

### INTERMEDIATE (6-18 months)
**Capabilities:**
- 12-20 reps or 30-60 second holds
- Mastered basic movement patterns
- Ready for complex variations

**Recommended Exercises:**
- Standard Push-ups, Diamond Push-ups
- Jump Squats, Bulgarian Split Squats
- Side Planks, Plank variations
- Standard Burpees
- Pull-ups/Chin-ups
- Walking/Lateral Lunges

---

### ADVANCED (18+ months)
**Capabilities:**
- 20+ reps or 60+ second holds
- Mastery of bodyweight control
- Ready for one-limb progressions

**Recommended Exercises:**
- One-arm Push-ups, Handstand Push-ups
- Pistol Squats, Shrimp Squats
- Planche Progressions, L-sit Hold
- Burpee Pull-ups, Burpee Box Jumps
- Muscle-ups, Archer Pull-ups
- Dragon Flags, Windshield Wipers

---

### Progression Methods
1. **Increase reps/time** - More repetitions or hold duration
2. **Change body position** - Adjust leverage (incline → standard → decline)
3. **Reduce stability** - Single-leg/single-arm variations
4. **Add tempo** - Slow down movements (time under tension)
5. **Increase ROM** - Deeper squats, deficit push-ups
6. **Add plyometrics** - Transform into explosive versions

## 5. HIIT Workout Formats

### TABATA (20s work / 10s rest)
**Structure:**
- 8 rounds
- 20 seconds work
- 10 seconds rest
- 4 minutes total per exercise

**Best for:** Maximum intensity, cardiovascular conditioning, fat burning

**Example:** 8 rounds of Burpees

---

### EMOM (Every Minute On the Minute)
**Structure:**
- Perform reps at start of each minute
- Rest remainder of minute
- 10-20 minutes total

**Best for:** Maintaining form quality, strength with cardio, controlled pacing

**Example:**
- Min 1: 10 Push-ups
- Min 2: 15 Squats
- Min 3: 20 Mountain Climbers
- Repeat 5 rounds (15 min)

---

### AMRAP (As Many Rounds As Possible)
**Structure:**
- Complete circuit as many times as possible
- Set time limit (10-30 minutes)
- Focus on volume

**Best for:** Building endurance, testing limits, mental toughness

**Example (15-min AMRAP):**
- 10 Push-ups
- 15 Squats
- 20 Mountain Climbers

---

### STANDARD CIRCUIT FORMATS

#### Traditional Circuit
- 8-12 stations
- 30-45s per exercise
- 15-30s rest between
- 2-3 rounds

#### Pyramid Circuit
- Reps increase then decrease (5-10-15-20-15-10-5)
- Good for endurance

#### Ladder Circuit
- Reps increase each round (5, 10, 15)
- Or decrease (reverse ladder)

#### Superset Circuit
- Pair opposing muscle groups
- Minimal rest between pairs
- 30-60s rest between supersets

#### Timed Rounds
- **40/20:** 40s work, 20s rest
- **45/15:** 45s work, 15s rest
- **50/10:** 50s work, 10s rest

---

### AQAP (As Quick As Possible)
**Structure:**
- Complete prescribed reps/rounds
- Record time as score

**Example:**
- 100 Burpees for time
- 5 rounds: 10 Push-ups, 15 Squats, 20 Mountain Climbers

---

### Specialty Formats

#### 21-15-9 (CrossFit style)
- 21 reps each exercise
- 15 reps each exercise
- 9 reps each exercise
- Complete for time

#### Death By...
- Min 1: 1 rep
- Min 2: 2 reps
- Min 3: 3 reps
- Continue until failure

#### Chipper
- Long list, high reps
- Work through once
- Example: 100 Squats, 90 Push-ups, 80 Sit-ups, 70 Lunges

## Programming Principles

### Exercise Selection
1. **Alternate muscle groups** - Upper, lower, core rotation
2. **Mix modalities** - Strength, cardio, plyometrics
3. **Progress systematically** - Master basics first
4. **Consider constraints** - Space, equipment, time

### Beginner Programming
- Rest periods: 30-45 seconds
- Rounds: 2-3
- Interval: 30-40 seconds work
- Focus: Form and technique

### Intermediate Programming
- Rest periods: 15-30 seconds
- Rounds: 3-4
- Interval: 20-30 seconds work
- Focus: Adding variations

### Advanced Programming
- Rest periods: 10-15 seconds
- Rounds: 4-6
- Interval: 20-30 seconds work
- Focus: Complex movements, max intensity
